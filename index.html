Level 0: The Creation

Pokemon has been around for decades, growing into an international sensation that captured the hearts of both milennials and youth. The idea stemmed from one man with an affinity for bugs, evolving into a popular media franchise famous for their role-playing video games. The franchise is currently at the 7th Generation, having significantly advanced from the grayscale, pixelated graphics of their very first game to 3D rendering and effects of the current generation's game.

To explore the evolution of Pokemon's main series of video games, move the character below using the left and right arrow keys!

1989

When Satoshi Tajiri was introduced to Game Boy, he imagined bugs being able to transmit between the wires. In 1989, both him and Ken Sugimori teamed up to form Game Freak Inc., pitching their idea to Nintendo which was originally called "Capsule Monsters". Though they did fail many times, they eventually brought in Shigeru Myamoto who successfully pitched the game to Nintendo and earned funding to develop the project for a total of six years. The name "Capsule Monsters" was eventually changed to "Pocket Monsters" throughout the development process.

Level 1: Red and Green

1996

On February 27, 1996, the first Pokemon games to ever be released came to the Nintendo Game Boy system in Japan. These games, Pokemon Red and Green, begin the video game series with Red, a ten-year old boy starting his journey as a Pokemon Trainer at the same time as his rival, Blue.

Game Platform: Game Boy

Generation: 1

Features

  • Gyms: a main feature of the continuing series, this feature introduced a badge system where one badge is earned after defeating a gym leader in the game's region for a total of 8 badges
  • Elite Four: a title given to four trainers that are proclaimed as the best in their region
  • 151 Pokemon

1998

On September 28, 1998, Pokemon Red and Blue were co-released to become available to North America. It follows the same plot as Japan's Red and Green versions, with the exception of additional exclusive Pokemon only obtainable through trading. Though released near the end of the Game Boy era, Red and Blue still earned the title of the best-selling role-playing games of all time right after their release.

Game Platform: Game Boy

Generation: 1

1999

Released on October 18, 1999, in North America, Pokemon Yellow was a special edition solitary version that came with minor changes and was inspired by the animation that was released in 1997. In this version, Pikachu is the only starter Pokemon the player can choose unlike the previous versions where the player had the option of choosing either Charmander, Squirtle, or Bulbasaur. Blue, Red's rival, ends up with Eevee as his starter pokemon.

Game Platform: Game Boy, Game Boy Color

Generation: 1

Level 2: Gold and Silver

2000

On October 15, 2000, Pokemon Gold and Silver were released to the public after a delay due to worn out developers. Gold and Silver take place in a different region called Johto whereas the previous games took place in the Kanto region.

Game Platform: Game Boy, Game Boy Color

Generation: 2

Features

  • The 151 Pokemon from Generation 1 plus 100 new Pokemon species
  • Storage of items such as Poke Balls, healing items, and key items in separte compartments
  • Battle screen shows how much experience points a Pokemon has until next level
  • Pokemon appearances influenced by time of day
  • Breeding
  • Two new Pokemon types introduced: Steel and Dark

2000

Following Pokemon Gold and Silver, Pokemon Crystal came out as a solitary version with minor changes similar to the releases of Red and Green/Red and Blue with Pokemon Yellow.

Game Platform: Game Boy Color

Generation: 2

Level 3: Ruby and Sapphire

2003

A whole new region is introduced with the release of Pokemon Ruby and Sapphire on March 19, 2003. The Hoenn region, as usual, contains 8 new gym leaders as well as four new Elite Four syndicates. A new protagonist is shown and the player is able to choose between Brendan, the male protagonist, or May, the female protagonist. The rival approach differs in this series. Instead of getting introduced to a new individual as your rival, the gender opposite to the player's chosen gender is the rival. The new plot follows a similar concept as the previous games as the player continues to defeat gym leaders and beat the Elite Four while interacting with new characters and striving to beat your rival/childhood friend.

Game Platform: Game Boy Advance

Generation: 3

Features

  • 386 Pokemon (151 from Generation 1, 100 from Generation 2, and 135 from this generation)
  • New Poke Balls such as the Premier Ball, Repeat Ball, Timer Ball, Nest Ball, Dive Ball, and Luxury Ball
  • Double Battles are now possible allowing the player to use two Pokemon at a time
  • Pokemon contests to show off skills of multiple categories (Beauty, Cool, Cute, Smart, and Tough) were added to promote player interactivity with their Pokemon
  • Breeding
  • New backgrounds including backgrounds with weather effects

2004

The Kanto region is revisited with the release of Pokemon FireRed and LeafGreen on September 9, 2004. They are the first remakes of the Pokemon franchise, bringing back the original characters, plot elements, and challenges from Pokemon Red and Green with a few new additions. The player can now choose a female protagonist as opposed to only being able to play the male protagonist in the original Red and Green versions.

Game Platform: Game Boy Advance

Generation: 3

Features

  • A new southern region called the Sevil Islands has been added and populated with Generation 2 Pokemon
  • Like Generation 3 games, Pokemon now have natures, abilities, and genders, and can hold items
  • When entering certain locations, single images of that location appear upon entering such as forests and caves

2005

Like Pokemon Yellow and Crystal, Pokemon Emerald is released as a sister game to Pokemon Ruby and Sapphire on May 1, 2005. In this version.

Game Platform: Game Boy Advance

Generation: 3

Features

  • Animated Pokemon sprites during battle
  • Encounters with Legendary Pokemon include a different animation before transitioning into the battle scene

Level 4: Diamond and Pearl

2007-2009

Pokemon Diamond and Pearl initiate the beginning of Generation 4 in the Pokemon series as it is released on April 22, 2007. The game introduces the main protagonists, Lucas and Dawn, and their rival, Barry in the Sinnoh region. As the player, once again, aims to battle all the gym leaders and defeat the Elite Four additional features are included that let the player further explore and interact with their environment.

Pokemon Platinum, like the previous series, is Diamond and Pearl's sister game. It was released on March 22, 2009 and occurs in the same region.

Game Platform: Nintendo DS

Generation: 4

Features

  • 107 new Pokemon, totaling to 493 Pokemon altogether
  • A Pokemon radar to interact with the gaming environment
  • Fight, Survival, and Resort Areas
  • Increasing battle skills at the Battle Tower
  • A real-time clock feature that changes the environment from day-time to night-time
  • A new battle system that declares attacks as either physical or special
  • Pokemon Super Contests where Pokemon are entered and judge in a Visual Competition and a Dance Competition

2010

On March 14, 2010, Pokemon HeartGold and SoulSilver was released as remakes of Generation 2's Pokemon Gold and Silver. Like Pokemon FireRed and LeafGreen and Pokemon Red and Green, the pair of games conjoin the generations that they both center around. In Pokemon HeartGold and SoulSilver, the player revisits Generation 1's Kanto region and retells the story of Johto. The overall plot follows the same direction as that in Pokemon Gold and Silver except the male protagonist is completely redesigned and called Ethan and a new female protagonist is introduced called Lyra.

Game Platform: Nintendo DS

Generation: 4

Features

  • Expansion of key areas
  • All 493 Pokemon are able to trail behind the player while they walk in the game
  • The player can interact with their Pokemon by pressing buttons on their gaming device
  • Ambient sounds are heavily emphasized
  • A redesign inthe Pokedex

Level 5: Black and White

2011

On March 6, 2011, the franchise released the first series of Generation 5: Pokemon Black and White. Unlike previous games, Pokemon prior to Generation 5 cannot be obtianed before completing the game's main story. New main characters are introduced: Hilbert and Hilda.

Game Platform: Nintendo DS, Nintendo DSi

Generation: 5

Features

  • 156 new Pokemon, bringing the overall total to 649
  • General graphical enhancements such as higher level of 3D graphics and camera angles
  • A game environment that includes seasons
  • Pokemon contests replaced with Pokemon Musicals which are similar to the Super Contests in Diamon, Pearl, and Platinum
  • Changing music based on battle circumstances
  • Triple battles: three Pokemon on each side sent out at once but the target opponent that a Pokemon can attack is restricted
  • Rotation battles: battles where three Pokemon on each team are sent onto each side however they are sent out onto a circular platform

2012

On October 7, 2012, Pokemon Black and White gained a new pairing to the fifth Generation of the series: Pokemon Black and White 2. The plot takes place two years after the events of Pokemon Black and White, thus leading to new accessible locations and an addition of new gym leaders. New main protagonists are introduced to this series: Nate and Rosa.

Game Platform: Nintendo DS, Nintendo DSi

Generation: 5

Features

  • A bar that shows a Pokemon's level, experience points, and HP has been redesigned to black
  • Several attacks have new battle animations
  • Battle menu has been redesigned
  • Each game character has a fully animated sprite

Level 6: X and Y

2013

On October 12, 2013, the player enters the Kalos region of the Pokemon world in Pokmeon X and Y. Main characters Calem and Serena are introduced along with new rivals Shauna, Trevor, and Tierno. This next evolution of the Pokemon series boasts 3D gameplay as it advances up in 3D graphics.

Game Platform: Nintendo 3DS

Generation: 6

Features

  • 3D modeled cel-shaded characters and Pokemon are used instead of the traditional sprites
  • More lively reactions to Pokemon attacks
  • 8 directional grid that allows for diagonal movement
  • Roller-skating as a method of transportation
  • Pokemon-Amie: allows for players to develop stronger bonds with their Pokemon by providing a touch screen for players to pet, feed, and play with the Pokemon currently on their team
  • Sky battles between flying Pokemon
  • Trainer customization: hair color and clothing can be changed

2014

On October 21, 2014, the release of Pokemon OmegaRuby and AlphaSapphire brings a new addition into the Generation 6 core series while remaking Generation 3's Ruby and Sapphire games. Just like the previous remake series, OmegaRuby and AlphaSapphire follow the same story plot as their original counterparts.

Game Platform: Nintendo 3DS

Generation: 6

Features

  • 3D modeled cel-shaded characters and Pokemon are used instead of the traditional sprites
  • More lively reactions to Pokemon attacks
  • 8 directional grid that allows for diagonal movement
  • Roller-skating as a method of transportation
  • Pokemon-Amie: allows for players to develop stronger bonds with their Pokemon by providing a touch screen for players to pet, feed, and play with the Pokemon currently on their team
  • Sky battles between flying Pokemon
  • Trainer customization: hair color and clothing can be changed

Level 7: Sun and Moon

2016

On November 18, 2016, Pokemon Sun and Moon is released to the public and becomes the starting series of Generation 7. Unlike many of the other main game series, Sun and Moon begins with a video call from the game's Professor. The interaction with the Professor helps create a Trainer Passport for the player before the player moves from the Kanto region to the new Alola region. Like every series, new characters and character designs are introduced.

Game Platform: Nintendo 3DS

Generation: 7

Features

  • 81 new Pokemon, bringing the overall total to 802 Pokemon
  • Rotom Pokedex: acts as a real-time map on the bottom screen on the player's Nintendo 3DS, showing the player's location, places of interest, and recommended destinations
  • QR Scanner: players can scan QR codes corresponding to each Pokemon in the Pokedex, letting them register them as Pokemon they have seen
  • Alola Forms: the Pokemon in this seras have uniquely adapted to the microclimates of the Alola region, consequently becoming a regional variant of their original species from other regions
  • Roller-skating as a method of transportation
  • Battle Royal: four players can enter into a free-for-all battle, with any player able to attack or help another
  • Poke Rides: players can call on the services of certain Pokemon in order to access areas they would not be able to on their own

2017

The most recent Pokemon game released, Pokemon UltraSun and UltraMoon, came out on November 17, 2017. They are the next paired versions with their counterparts in Generation 7 and feature an alternate storyline from the original Pokemon Sun and Moon. The beginning of the game, however, follows the same route as Pokemon Sun and Moon in which the player is in a video call with the same Professor to finalize a Trainer Passport.

Game Platform: Nintendo 3DS

Generation: 7

Features

  • 5 new Pokemon, bringing the overall total to 807 Pokemon
  • Animations leading to battles flash in colors of the rainbow
  • Some locations feature visible wild Pokemon wandering in the background
  • The player can now take photos with the Pokemon in their party as well as add stickers to the photos
